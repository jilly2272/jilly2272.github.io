About Me

Programmers and narrative writers have nothing in common. One is a technical discipline that utilises logic, while the other is artistic and is built on emotion. Programmers aren't creative, and writers aren't technical - right? I have always considered myself to be a creative and technical person. Growing up in my home country of Azerbaijan, I loved science just as much as the arts, so I found myself in a difficult position when deciding what I wanted to study at university. Meanwhile, I started falling in love with gaming (tabletop and digital), so I thankfully didn't have to choose one field over the other as I studied a BSc in Interactive Media where I learnt to program and design video games. In terms of my technical experience, I have 3 years of C# .NET and Unity, predominately with UI, feature development, and QA and testing in a mobile (IOS and Android) and desktop app. On the writing front, I have created close to 1000 cards for board games, written pitch documents and scripts for a video game writing course, and published an online puzzle escape room! To answer my initial question, I don't think programming and writing are that different. Programming requires creativity to solve complex or unique problems while writing and design require critical thinking to construct clear and satisfying narratives. Together, they offer a unique lens through which to examine projects, and I want to share my experience with you. If you need someone who can think outside the box and likes to solve problems, get in touch and check out my portfolio!
